Our Journey

"No act of kindness, no matter how small, is ever wasted."

  • 2009-2011

    Our Humble Beginnings

    At first we four friends thought of helping out poor kids in our locality also we used to give lessons in nearby slums every Sunday mornings.Later on as we grew up we decided to start an organisation and formed a team of 20 alike. In the beginning we served only Maharastra for two years and progressed eventualy.

  • 2011-2014

    An Agency is Born

    Later on our connections grew up and were able to expand our social services.We raised good amount of funds with help of fundraisings and served 4 neighbouring states too.We were on cloud nine, serving almost 1200 children and were looking forward for more opportunities.

  • 2014-2016

    Tremendous Growth

    As time passed by we had a large team with us. Now we also started giving employment to the poor and reached out to each and everyone who needed any kind of help.We had our NGO serving 7 states helping about 2500 children and gave employment to 500 individuals.

  • 2016-2019

    At our best

    Being an organisation growing at such a pace reaching out to everyone who needed our help we had almost reached our goals but for sure wanted to go on and on expanding our reach. We also held several campaigns in the last two years benefitting a number of people in various ways. We also started small educational institues in remote villages at several places.We aim to reach every single individual who needs us.

Homeless to stardom

The incredible success story of Shreeshant...

Shreeshant lived with his ailing mother in a bus stand, living off of the streets and the kindness of strangers. After his mother’s death, Shreeshant was left alone and had nowhere to go. For young Shreeshant, the future seemed bleak and desolate. This was until we found Shreeshant and decided to take him under our wings. Shreeshant showed a lot of promise and excelled in several fields including sports and acting. He developed a keen interest particularly towards dancing and acting. He was encouraged to participate and showcase his skills at various events and was also made a part of numerous street plays and short films. After he finished his 10th, special arrangements were made to train Shreeshant to hone his skills and talent. During one such training, Shreeshant had the opportunity to audition for a Kannada TV serial and was selected. Shreeshant now plays the lead role in the serial ‘Shani’ which broadcasts regularly on all weekdays on the ‘Colour Kannada’ channel. Shreeshant is now one of the most celebrated TV stars in Karnataka.

  • Date: February 2012
  • Funds Provided:$80,000

NOTHING CAN STOP YOU

Promising Steps Towards A New Milestone

Ganesh had delayed milestones early on in his childhood and was later diagnosed as mentally retarded with 75 % level disability. He was unruly, screamed a lot, broke things in the house and threw frequent tantrums. He was unable to speak or respond to even simple instructions. His mother went through a very difficult life with no support or money. With Ganesh requiring constant care and monitoring, she could not take on any job since there was no one to look after Ganesh. Having heard about ZeroTear from her neighbor, she approached the NGO for employment as a domestic maid hoping she would be able to work and take care of Ganesh at the same time. Not only was she given the job, she was also offered training as a caregiver and Ganesh was admitted in the Centre. This was a major turning point for both Ganesh and his mother. Ganesh was provided with special education and therapy and has shown remarkable improvement ever since. He speaks well and is physically very active now. With ZeroTear’s help, he was able to get a Disability Pension from the government. His mother is paid a decent salary with food and clothing for both of them and they are now able to live peacefully and comfortably.

  • Date: March 2013
  • Funds Provided: $1,20,000

HOPE WINS

Where there is a way there is a way...Never Give Up

Aakruti was just ten years old when her parents died of severe illness. Due to poverty they were unable to go to the local hospital. The little girl was left alone in this unfamiliar world with her younger sister. Soon they moved to their relatives’ house but the children were badly treated by them. Aakruti took her younger sister and started living on the street. After two years Aakruti’s maternal aunt came to know about their situation and brought the siblings with her. The aunt worked as bonded labour and earned less than Rupees 50 per day. Despite her willingness to support children, poverty forced her to look out for alternate ways. She came to know about ZeroTear from Child Welfare Committee Anantapur and decided to send the siblings. “I was scared at the idea of going and living in a new place but Aunty assured us that it is the best place for needy children,” said Aakruti. ZeroTear welcomed Aakruti and her sister warmly. She introduced them to their ZeroTear brothers and sisters and showed their room. The children were happy to see a clean room with bed and some toys. Aakruti was cheerful and wanted to play with her toys. She innocently told , “I am holding a toy for the first time in my life.” Since Aakruti had never been to school, she was given some extra coaching before she could begin formal school. Today, she regularly goes to school and actively participates in extracurricular activities.She is ambititous and looking forward to becoming a doctor and treat everyone without any fee.

  • Date: November 2016
  • Funds Provided:$75,000
